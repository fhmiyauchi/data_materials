# DEFINITIONS

## MATERIAL SCIENCE

Materials Science is the study of how materials are structured, how they behave, and how to design or improve them for practical use.

It connects:
- Physics → mechanical, electrical, thermal behavior
- Chemistry → composition and reactions
- Engineering → manufacturing and applications

The field studies materials such as:
- Metals
- Polymers (plastics)
- Ceramics
- Semiconductors
- Composites
- Biomaterials
- Nanomaterials

Typical questions in materials science:
- Why does steel become stronger after heat treatment?
- How can batteries charge faster?
- Which material is best for a lightweight aircraft?
- How do we make computer chips smaller and faster?
- How can we create biodegradable packaging?

Common applications:
- EV batteries
- Aerospace
- Electronics
- Construction
- Medical implants
- Renewable energy
- Sports equipment

A core idea in materials science is the relationship between:

Structure → Processing → Properties → Performance

Meaning:
- How a material is made changes its internal structure.
- Structure determines properties.
- Properties determine real-world performance.

Example:
- Carbon arranged one way becomes graphite (soft).
- Carbon arranged another way becomes diamond (extremely hard).


## MATERIAL INTELLIGENCE - AI + Materials Science

“Material Intelligence” is a newer and less formally standardized term. Using:
- Machine Learning
- AI
- Large datasets
- Simulations
to discover or optimize materials faster.

This is becoming a major field because traditional materials discovery can take years or decades.
Examples:
- Predicting new battery chemistries
- Discovering stronger alloys
- Designing heat-resistant materials for aerospace
- Finding better semiconductors
- Accelerating drug-delivery biomaterials

Companies and research labs use:
- AI models
- Molecular simulations
- Quantum calculations
- High-throughput experimentation
to reduce experimentation time dramatically.

This area overlaps with:
- Materials Informatics
- Computational materials science
- AI for chemistry
- Digital twins (IoT + AI + Big Data)
    - Compnent Twin
    - Asset Twin (entire machine / system)
    - System Twin (multiple assets)
    - Process Twin (workflows / Operations)
